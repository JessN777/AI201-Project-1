Club World Cup host cities ranked by atmosphere
💬Discussion
Went to matches in three different cities — here's my honest ranking of the atmospheres.

1. Miami — The Inter Miami matches here were electric. The crowd was insanely loud, huge number of Messi fans who drove hours to be there. The heat was brutal though.

2. New York / New Jersey (MetLife) — Best stadium of the tournament. The size alone is intimidating and when it's full for a Real Madrid match you can feel it in your chest. Parking situation is a disaster.

3. Los Angeles (SoFi Stadium) — Beautiful venue, great screens, good food options. The crowd was a bit quieter though — felt more like a concert than a football match in some games.

4. Seattle — Honestly underrated. CenturyLink (Lumen Field) is a proper football atmosphere and the fans were genuinely into it even for games without big name clubs.

5. Philadelphia — Gets the job done but felt a bit corporate. Best public transport option of any host city though.

6. Boston / Foxborough — The drive out to Gillette Stadium from the city is brutal. Stadium itself is fine but not special.

Overall the tournament organizers did a solid job. Considering this is also a dry run for the 2026 World Cup it's a good sign.
