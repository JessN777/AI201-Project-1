Mbappe leads Real Madrid to Club World Cup glory
📰News
Kylian Mbappe delivered a stunning hat-trick as Real Madrid defeated Manchester City 4-2 in the FIFA Club World Cup final in New York. It was a performance many had been waiting for since his blockbuster move to the Spanish giants.

Mbappe opened the scoring in the 12th minute with a trademark left-footed strike, then doubled the lead before halftime. City pulled two back through Erling Haaland, but Mbappe completed his hat-trick in extra time to seal the trophy.

Carlo Ancelotti praised his striker after the match: "Kylian was unstoppable tonight. This is why we brought him to Madrid."

Real Madrid becomes the first club to win the new 32-team Club World Cup format, adding to their already legendary trophy cabinet. The tournament drew record attendance across all US host cities.
