African clubs making their mark at the Club World Cup
📰News
One of the more surprising storylines of the 2025 FIFA Club World Cup has been the performance of African clubs. Both Al Ahly (Egypt) and Mamelodi Sundowns (South Africa) have shown they belong on the world stage.

Al Ahly:
- Held Inter Miami to a draw in their opening match
- Their organized defensive structure frustrated Messi for nearly 70 minutes
- Coach Marcel Koller said: "We showed the world that CAF football is not here to make up numbers"

Mamelodi Sundowns:
- Qualified from a tough group that included PSG and Atletico Madrid
- Striker Iqraam Rayners scored 3 goals in the group stage
- Became only the second African club after Al Ahly to advance to the knockout rounds

The performances have reignited the debate about African football development and whether more African clubs deserve spots in future editions of the tournament. FIFA currently allocates 4 spots to CAF, and there are growing calls to expand that to 6.

It's a genuine feel-good story and has earned respect from fans and pundits worldwide.
